▶ How does this work? What is the algorithm actually doing?

Your O/M/P estimate captures things you cannot control — how long tasks actually take, whether requirements change, whether the unexpected happens. These are uncertainties. The distribution is anchored to your judgment.

The 7 management conditions are different. They capture decisions you make as a manager: how much budget headroom to maintain, how frozen the scope is, how much rework to plan for. These are not uncertain — they are choices you control.

The SACO algorithm searches thousands of combinations of those 7 conditions to find which specific set gives you the best probability at your target. It does not change what is uncertain about the project. It finds which set of conditions, if genuinely implemented, shifts the odds the most. Think of it as a policy search engine, not a prediction engine.

One guardrail: the algorithm will never recommend a stance so extreme that it contradicts your own O/M/P judgment. A KL-divergence constraint keeps the result anchored to your estimate.
